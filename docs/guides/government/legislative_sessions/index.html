Legislative Sessions

Legislative Sessions#

Legislative Sessions are where bills & motions get submitted to. They can be openend by either the Speaker or Vice-Speaker, and usually last a few days, but there are no time limits anywhere. Session are divided into periods. Every legislative session starts in the Submission Period, where bills & motions can freely be submitted.

note

Depending on current laws in the nation we're playing as, who can submit bills & motions may vary. For example, the Legislature often allows everyone (read: citizens) to submit bills, but restricts the ability to submit motions to just Legislators.

Once the Speaker feels like enough time has passed for people to submit their bills and motions, they can lock submissions by doing either one of these options:

  1. If you want to stop submissions coming in, but aren't ready yet to start voting, you can lock the session with -parliament session lock. You can allow submissions to be submitted again by unlocking the session with -parliament session unlock.

  2. (Optional) Set the session into Voting Period with -parliament session vote. The only advantage of setting a session into Voting Period before directly closing it, is that I will DM every legislator a reminder to vote and the link to the voting form, and the voting form will be displayed in -parliament session. After enough time has passed for everyone to vote, you would close the session as described in the next step.

  3. Close the session entirely with -parliament session close.

note

The Voting Period is optional.

  1. -legislature session open a. Everyone can submit bills and motions with -legislature submit b. Bills and motions can be withdrawn again, the Speaker/Vice-Speaker can withdraw every bill/motion, while everyone else can only withdraw the bills/motions they submitted

  2. [Optional Step] -legislature session vote google forms voting form a. Sets session into Voting Period. No one can submit bills/motions anymore. b. Legislators get DM with the Google Forms voting form link c. Google Forms voting form can be generated by the bot itself, with -legislature session export d. Actual voting takes place outside of the bot and is up to you how to do it! The bot only cares about the end result of the votes.

  3. -legislature session close a. No one can submit bills/motions anymore.

  4. -legislature pass the_bills_that_passed a. Mark the bills that passed from the session as passed b. If Ministry Veto exists: i. Bills get sent to Ministry c. If Ministry Veto does not exist: i. Bills are passed into law. ii. Bills are now in -laws and -laws search.

  5. (Only Maori Bot) -legislature superpass bills a. Pass the bills that received a super-majority directly into law to skip the Ariki vote

Executive Veto (Only @Maori and @Canada)

  1. -ministry bills a. See the bills that passed recently from the Legislature

  2. -ministry pass bills a. Pass bills into law. b. Bills are now in -laws and -laws search.

  3. -ministry veto bills a. Veto bills. b. Veto can be overridden by the Legislature with -legislature override c. Bills that were vetoed can be re-submitted to the next legislative session with -legislature bill resubmit bills
